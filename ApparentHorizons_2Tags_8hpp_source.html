256 /// `NormalOneForm(i)` is \f$s_i\f$, the (unnormalized) normal one-form
257 /// to the surface, expressed in Cartesian components.
258 /// This is computed by \f$x_i/r-\partial r_{\rm surf}/\partial x^i\f$,
259 /// where \f$x_i/r\f$ is `Rhat` and

262 /// Note on the word "normal": \f$s_i\f$ points in the correct direction
263 /// (it is "normal" to the surface), but it does not have unit length
264 /// (it is not "normalized"; normalization requires a metric).

370 /// `Tangents(i,j)` is \f$\partial x_{\rm surf}^i/\partial q^j\f$,
371 /// where \f$x_{\rm surf}^i\f$ are the Cartesian coordinates of the
372 /// surface (i.e. `CartesianCoords`) and are considered functions of
373 /// \f$(\theta,\phi)\f$.
374 ///
375 /// \f$\partial/\partial q^0\f$ means
376 /// \f$\partial/\partial\theta\f$; and \f$\partial/\partial q^1\f$
377 /// means \f$\csc\theta\,\,\partial/\partial\phi\f$. Note that the
378 /// vectors `Tangents(i,0)` and `Tangents(i,1)` are orthogonal to the
379 /// `NormalOneForm` \f$s_i\f$, i.e.
380 /// \f$s_i \partial x_{\rm surf}^i/\partial q^j = 0\f$; this statement
381 /// is independent of a metric. Also, `Tangents(i,0)` and
382 /// `Tangents(i,1)` are not necessarily orthogonal to each other,
383 /// since orthogonality between 2 vectors (as opposed to a vector and
384 /// a one-form) is metric-dependent.
385 template <typename Frame>

387  using type = aliases::Jacobian<Frame>;
388 };
