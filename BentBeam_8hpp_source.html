27 /*!
28  * \brief A state of pure bending of an elastic beam in 2D
29  *
30  * \details This solution describes a 2D slice through an elastic beam of length
31  * \f$L\f$ and height \f$H\f$, centered around (0, 0), that is subject to a
32  * bending moment \f$M=\int T^{xx}y\mathrm{d}y\f$ (see e.g.
33  * \cite ThorneBlandford2017, Eq. 11.41c for a bending moment in 1D). The beam
34  * material is characterized by an isotropic and homogeneous constitutive
35  * relation \f$Y^{ijkl}\f$ in the plane-stress approximation (see
36  * `Elasticity::ConstitutiveRelations::IsotropicHomogeneous`). In this scenario,
37  * no body-forces \f$f_\mathrm{ext}^j\f$ act on the material, so the
38  * \ref Elasticity equations reduce to \f$\nabla_i T^{ij}=0\f$, but the bending
39  * moment \f$M\f$ generates the stress
40  *
41  * \f{align}
42  * T^{xx} &= \frac{12 M}{H^3} y \\
43  * T^{xy} &= 0 = T^{yy} \text{.}
44  * \f}
45  *
46  * By fixing the rigid-body motions to
47  *
48  * \f[
49  * \xi^x(0,y)=0 \quad \text{and} \quad \xi^y\left(\pm \frac{L}{2},0\right)=0
50  * \f]
51  *
52  * we find that this stress is produced by the displacement field
53  *
54  * \f{align}
55  * \xi^x&=-\frac{12 M}{EH^3}xy \\
56  * \xi^y&=\frac{6 M}{EH^3}\left(x^2+\nu y^2-\frac{L^2}{4}\right)
57  * \f}
58  *
59  * in terms of the Young's modulus \f$E\f$ and the Poisson ration \f$\nu\f$ of
60  * the material. The corresponding strain \f$S_{ij}=\partial_{(i}\xi_{j)}\f$ is
61  *
62  * \f{align}
63  * S_{xx} &= -\frac{12 M}{EH^3} y \\
64  * S_{yy} &= \frac{12 M}{EH^3} \nu y \\
65  * S_{xy} &= S_{yx} = 0
66  * \f}
67  *
68  * and the potential energy stored in the entire infinitesimal slice is
69  *
70  * \f[
71  * \int_{-L/2}^{L/2} \int_{-H/2}^{H/2} U dy\,dx = \frac{6M^2}{EH^3}L \text{.}
72  * \f]
73  */
