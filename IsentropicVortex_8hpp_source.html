35 /*!
36  * \brief Newtonian isentropic vortex in Cartesian coordinates
37  *
38  * The analytic solution to the 2-D Newtonian Euler system
39  * representing the slow advection of an incompressible, isentropic
40  * vortex \cite Yee1999. The initial condition is the superposition of a
41  * mean uniform flow with a gaussian-profile vortex. When embedded in
42  * 3-D space, the isentropic vortex is still a solution to the corresponding 3-D
43  * system if the velocity along the third axis is a constant. In Cartesian
44  * coordinates \f$(x, y, z)\f$, and using dimensionless units, the primitive
45  * quantities at a given time \f$t\f$ are then
46  *
47  * \f{align*}
48  * \rho &= \left[1 - \dfrac{(\gamma - 1)\beta^2}{8\gamma\pi^2}\exp\left(
49  * 1 - r^2\right)\right]^{1/(\gamma - 1)}, \\
50  * v_x &= U - \dfrac{\beta\tilde y}{2\pi}\exp\left(\dfrac{1 - r^2}{2}\right),\\
51  * v_y &= V + \dfrac{\beta\tilde x}{2\pi}\exp\left(\dfrac{1 - r^2}{2}\right),\\
52  * v_z &= W,\\
53  * \epsilon &= \frac{\rho^{\gamma - 1}}{\gamma - 1},
54  * \f}
55  *
56  * with
57  *
58  * \f{align*}
59  * r^2 &= {\tilde x}^2 + {\tilde y}^2,\\
60  * \tilde x &= x - X_0 - U t,\\
61  * \tilde y &= y - Y_0 - V t,
62  * \f}
63  *
64  * where \f$(X_0, Y_0)\f$ is the position of the vortex on the \f$(x, y)\f$
65  * plane at \f$t = 0\f$, \f$(U, V, W)\f$ are the components of the mean flow
66  * velocity, \f$\beta\f$ is the vortex strength, and \f$\gamma\f$ is the
67  * adiabatic index. The pressure \f$p\f$ is then obtained from the dimensionless
68  * polytropic relation
69  *
70  * \f{align*}
71  * p = \rho^\gamma.
72  * \f}
73  *
74  * On the other hand, if the velocity along the \f$z-\f$axis is not a constant
75  * but a function of the \f$z\f$ coordinate, the resulting modified isentropic
76  * vortex is still a solution to the Newtonian Euler system, but with source
77  * terms that are proportional to \f$dv_z/dz\f$. (See
78  * NewtonianEuler::Sources::VortexPerturbation.) For testing purposes,
79  * we choose to write the velocity as a uniform field plus a periodic
80  * perturbation,
81  *
82  * \f{align*}
83  * v_z(z) = W + \epsilon \sin{z},
84  * \f}
85  *
86  * where \f$\epsilon\f$ is the amplitude of the perturbation. The resulting
87  * source for the Newtonian Euler system will then be proportional to
88  * \f$\epsilon \cos{z}\f$.
89  */
