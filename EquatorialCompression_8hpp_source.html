23 /*!
24  * \ingroup CoordinateMapsGroup
25  *
26  * \brief Redistributes gridpoints on the sphere.
27  * \image html EquatorialCompression.png "A sphere with an `aspect_ratio` of 3."
28  *
29  * \details A mapping from the sphere to itself which depends on a single
30  * parameter, the `aspect_ratio` \f$\alpha\f$, which is the ratio of the
31  * horizontal length to the vertical height for a given point. This parameter
32  * name was chosen because points with \f$\tan \theta = 1\f$ get mapped to
33  * points with \f$\tan \theta' = \alpha\f$. In general, gridpoints located
34  * at an angle \f$\theta\f$ from the pole are mapped to a new angle
35  * \f$\theta'\f$ satisfying \f$\tan \theta' = \alpha \tan \theta\f$.
36  *
37  * For an `aspect_ratio` greater than one, the gridpoints are mapped towards
38  * the equator, leading to an equatorially compressed grid. For an
39  * `aspect_ratio` less than one, the gridpoints are mapped towards the poles.
40  * Note that the aspect ratio must be positive.
41  *
42  * We define the auxiliary variables \f$ r := \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 +z^2}\f$
43  * and \f$ \rho := \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + \alpha^{-2} z^2}\f$.
44  *
45  * The map corresponding to this transformation in cartesian coordinates
46  * is then given by:
47  *
48  * \f[\vec{x}'(x,y,z) =
49  * \frac{r}{\rho}\begin{bmatrix}
50  * x\\
51  * y\\
52  * \alpha^{-1} z\\
53  * \end{bmatrix}\f]
54  *
55  */
