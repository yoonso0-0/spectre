Detailed Description

Computes the leading part of \(\Psi_1\) near \(\mathcal I^+\).

Details

The value \(\Psi_1\) scales asymptotically as \(r^{-4}\), and has the form (in the coordinates used for regularity preserving CCE)

\begin{align*} \Psi_1^{(4)} = \frac{1}{8} \left(- 12 \eth \beta^{(2)} + J^{(1)} \bar Q^{(1)} + 2 Q^{(2)}\right) \end{align*}

where \(A^{(n)}\) is the \(1/r^n\) part of \(A\) evaluated at \(\mathcal I^+\), so for any quantity \(A\),

\begin{align*} \eth A^{(2)} &= (2 R^2 \eth \partial_y^2 A + 2 R \eth R \partial^2_y A)|_{y = 1}, \notag\\ A^{(1)} &= (- 2 R \partial_y A)|_{y = 1}, \notag\\ A^{(2)} &= (2 R^2 \partial_y^2 A)|_{y = 1}, \end{align*}

where the expansion is determined by the conversion between Bondi and numerical radii \(r = 2 R / (1 - y)\).
